FIFA 21 dataset: Data cleaning and transfomration and Exploratory data analysis

In this FIFA 21 dataset project, our primary aim is to extract valuable insights. Initially, our focus involves thorough data cleaning and transformation processes to augment our perspective and maximize the utility of columns for obtaining new insights.

Data Cleaning and Transformation:

- Are there columns devoid of value for our analysis that should be removed?

- Do the height and weight columns possess appropriate data types?

- Can we split the 'joined' column into year, month, and day columns?

- How might we clean and transform 'value', 'wage', and 'release clause' columns into integer columns?

- What methods can be employed to remove newline characters from the 'Club' column?

Exploratory Data Analysis:

- Which positions yield higher pay rates?

- Who are the undervalued players commanding low wages but exhibiting high value?

- Which countries should club managers focus on for scouting potential talent?

Conclusions

- The metrics encompass maximum value, mean, median, and minimum value. Given the significant disparity between the mean and the median values, it can be inferred that there are numerous outliers (values much higher or lower than the median). ,Messi has a wage of 560,000, which is 140 times higher than the median wage of 4,000.

- We can observe that the position with the highest average pay is CF (Center Forward)

- A summary of countries recommended for scouting talented players across different positions:

  1. Spain stands out for nurturing talented players in the ST, CDM, LB, CM, and RM positions.
  2. France holds the record for the highest average OVA among its players in the CB, CAM, CDM, and RB positions compared to the other 11 countries.
  3. Argentina is also a favorable choice for scouting players in the RM position.
  4. Germany possesses the highest number of players with the best average OVA in the CAM positions.
  5. Brazil is a noteworthy consideration for scouting players in the LM and RM positions.
